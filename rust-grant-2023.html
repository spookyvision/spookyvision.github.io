I've been writing software professionally for 24 years, been around various bushes (Perl, PHP, Python, Java, C#, C, C++, Ruby, Objective-C, Go, JS, TS, Flash) developing mainly web backend and frontend applications, iOS and Android apps, and embedded projects. After I fell in love with Rust I came up with the elevator pitch "Rust makes me sleep at night", because I've always been super anxious about some kind of bug I introduced would crash a BBC live TV show, the German Uber clone FreeNow, or a real-time trading platform with daily volumes in the millions of dollars (all real projects I've been involved with in a senior or lead position).

How long you have been involved with Rust

I learned Rust in 2018 for Advent Of Code, then used it mainly for various embedded projects and worked full time as trainer and senior developer for a year at Ferrous Systems (Jan 2021-2022). One particular highlight of my work at Ferrous: I was the sole developer of the initial public version of the Embedded Rust Trainings for Espressif training course.

Since then I've gone back to Freelancing and involving myself with community support.
