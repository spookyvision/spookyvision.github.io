About myself

I've been writing software professionally for 24 years, been around various bushes (Perl, PHP, Python, Java, C#, C, C++, Ruby, Objective-C, Go, JS, TS, Flash) developing mainly web backend and frontend applications, iOS and Android apps, and embedded projects. How long you have been involved with Rust

One particular highlight of my work at Ferrous: I was the sole developer of the initial public version of the Embedded Rust Trainings for Espressif training course.

Since then I've gone back to Freelancing and involving myself with community support.

Please tell us about how you propose supporting the Rust project during your Fellowship year and what the benefit of your work will be for the Rust project and the wider community of users

I am most passionate about embedded Rust, and think that it is still pretty underused both in hobbyist and professional spheres.

Previously I have worked with Arduino/PlatformIO and MicroPython/CircuitPython, and on some levels the Rust experience is clearly superior, but in many areas still lacking. In particular I've always liked the interactive exploration of a Python REPL and the rich tooling and community content of Arduino. Inspired by these, I have created several projects of my own, among these:

  • trenchcoat, a virtual machine that runs on embedded devices and in the browser, allowing instant code updates without a compile-flash-restart cycle

  • embedded web UI, a browser-based UI to send commands to an embedded device and also receive data, e.g. for browser console logging, live display of accelerometer chart data, or a real-time FFT

My goal is to further explore improvements in embedded Rust tooling by continuing work on these projects, developing of migration guides for people curious about Rust coming from other embedded platforms, and exploring further directions by doing case studies & interviews. James Munns' easier embedded Rust (which already features contributions by me) will serve as a starting point.

As one concrete example, Arduino has an integrated plotting tool whose functionality is pretty similar to part of what embedded-web-ui can provide, but it's far more polished than what I have to offer - for now!

For interactive/explorative development I'm planning to build an actual on-device REPL, evaluating rhai as potential alternative to trenchcoat.

I strongly believe the Browser/WebAssembly platform is an excellent companion platform to embedded development - a lot of embedded crates can be recompiled to WASM without any changes, allowing users to experiment without having to wire up sensors, lights, debug probes etc., and of course without the risk of causing hardware damage. I'm looking forward to enriching the ecosystem here - while wokwi is already an excellent existing and free choice, it is not entirely open source and thus cannot be run indepently from the wokwi website. Also its approach (emulating a microcontroller and running on top of that) is by its nature less performant than rebuilding everything for WASM, so it appears to me that simulating high-speed signals would break wokwi, but could be made possible with a different platform.
